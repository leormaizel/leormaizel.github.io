Prophia MVP From Zero to One

Background

Today, Prophia (FKA Leaseable) is a SaaS platform that enables Commercial Real Estate (CRE) firms to gain better insight into the performance of their portfolios.

When I joined as employee #2, Prophia was nothing but a hypothesis: the inefficiency endemic to CRE represents an opportunity for technological innovation. But what form that might take was unknown.

Validating the Problem Space

Before we built anything, the founding team met with 12 CRE firms ranging from boutique to REITs and pre-sold a hypothetical product to see which value propositions garnered the most interest.

  • Lease Management: making the day-to-day management of their tenants more efficient by automating manual processes
  • Transactional Efficiency: enabling CRE firms to track deals, negotiations, and collaborate as a team
  • Data Insights: providing authoritative benchmarking and market comp data

With findings from our interviews, we developed a working understanding of:

  • The organizational differences between CRE firms of different sizes
  • The job roles within each firm type

  • The opportunities untapped in the competitive landscape
  • The inter-organizational complexity of our ideas. I.e. some solutions could deliver value to just the CRE firm, whereas others required adoption lawyers, brokers, and banks as well

MVP Strategy

We focused our MVP on addressing the following key business problems:

#1: Critical business data is inaccessible

There is no source of business data more critical than the lease document; it drives cash flows, budget projections, buying/selling strategy, etc. However, this critical asset is always either a physical document locked in a fireproof cabinet or a PDF in a jumbled share drive. The data is neither accessible, extensible, nor dynamic

Furthermore, leases are inherently complex; landlords must deploy massive resources to create standardized summaries of the key points of the lease to mitigate this complexity.

#2: No single source of truth

An entire solar system of brittle reporting revolves around the lease document; Excel reports are pockmarked with broken references; uncertainty reigns over which report is up-to-date; data is frequently inconsistent between vendor software systems like accounting, budgeting, etc.

#3: Endless low-level busywork

CRE firms have rigorous reporting obligations to stakeholders such as lenders, capital partners, limited partners, the SEC, etc. Analysts regularly generate reports cobbled together from disparate systems and legal documents —for every single building.

In addition, the daily, bread-and-butter work of managing leases falls to the armies of lease administrators who must wade through complex legalese to determine the Landlord's obligations.

MVP Features

Given the problems we hoped to solve, the Chief Architect, CPO, and I brainstormed the key features of our MVP:

  • A digitized version of each lease contract, keyword searchable, where the key provisions of the lease are marked and standardized
  • A process that enables our internal team to capture each data point in each document
  • A standardized and accessible “lease summary” in which the most critical data is summarized
  • A searchable database of tenants
  • Canned industry-standard reports based on this data

Design Process

I’d never designed anything from scratch before; in every previous role, the universe of possibilities had already been collapsed into an existing IA/UI. But Prophia had zero constraints—an experience simultaneously nerve-wracking and liberating.

Several of my key considerations:

  • What are the key pieces of content and their differentiators? Is this content hierarchical in nature, or flat?
  • What are the key anticipated user paths throughout this content?

I sought to follow closely the paradigms that I observed were familiar to those in CRE, knowing that none of my designs would fully stand the test of time and that future designers would invariably curse my name.

Feedback from Design Partners

Prophia is an enterprise-level SaaS product; It isn’t cheap, it can’t be used instantly upon sign-up, and it involves long sales cycles. The Prophia MVP could not have succeeded without our Design Partners—8 CRE firms of different sizes. I ran (and continue to run) this program, geared toward:

  • Gathering feedback on features in design and on our roadmap
  • Shadowing team members to better understand workflows/processes
  • Access to sample critical business reports and documents

The Prophia MVP was released on September 18th, 2018. My ongoing research and validation work with Design Partners continues to inform our product prioritization, and we’ve seen steady quarterly growth in new customers and expansions ever since.

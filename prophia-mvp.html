Prophia MVP From Zero to One

Background

Prophia (FKA Leaseable) is a platform that enables Commercial Real Estate (CRE) firms to aggregate, manage, and leverage unstructured lease data to drive better portfolio performance.

When I joined as employee #2, none of this was true—yet. The founders had only a hypothesis: the inefficiency inherent in CRE processes represents an opportunity for technological innovation. But what form that should take was unknown.

Validating the Problem Space

Before we built anything, our founding team met with 12 CRE executives from firms ranging from boutique to REITs and pre-sold a hypothetical product to see which value propositions garnered the most interest.

  • Lease Management: giving CRE firms better insight into the contents of their lease portfolios
  • Transactional Efficiency: enabling CRE firms to track deals, lease negotiations, and collaborate as a team
  • Data Insights: providing authoritative benchmarking/market comp data

MVP Strategy

We focused our MVP on addressing the following key business problems:

#1: Critical business data is inaccessible

There is no source of business data more critical than the lease document; it drives cash flows, budget projections, buying/selling strategy, etc. However, this critical asset is always either a physical document locked in a fireproof cabinet or a PDF in a jumbled share drive. The data is neither accessible, extensible, nor dynamic

Furthermore, leases are inherently complex; landlords must employ massive resources to create standardized summaries of the key points of the lease to mitigate this complexity.

#2: No single source of truth

Since lease data is locked away in dumb, lifeless documents, an entire solar system of brittle reporting revolves around it; Excel reports are pockmarked with broken references; uncertainty reigns over which report is the most up-to-date; there are frequent data discrepancies between vendor software systems like accounting, cash-flow projection, etc., where no one can be sure of the correct figures without looking at the physical document.

#3: Endless low-level busywork

CRE firms have burdensome reporting obligations to stakeholders such as lenders, capital partners, limited partners, the SEC, etc. Analysts regularly need to generate critical reports cobbled together from numerous disparate systems, and from the legal documents themselves—for every single building the firm owns!

In addition, the daily, bread-and-butter work of managing leases falls to the armies of lease administrators who dig through the complex legalese of these documents to determine legal obligations.

MVP Features

Given the target problems we hoped to solve, the Chief Architect, CPO, and I brainstormed the key features of our MVP:

  • A digitized version of each lease contract, where the document is keyword searchable, and where the key provisions of the lease are marked and summarized
  • A process that enables our internal team to mark and annotate each data point in each document
  • A standardized and accessible “lease summary” in which the most critical data is summarized
  • A searchable database of tenants
  • Canned industry-standard reports based on this data

Design Process

I’d never designed anything from scratch before; in every previous role, the universe of endless possibilities had already been collapsed into an existing IA/UI. But designing Prophia was an exercise in having zero constraints—an experience simultaneously nerve-wracking and liberating.

Several of the key questions I considered were:

  • What are the key pieces of content on the app? What are their primary differentiators? Are they hierarchical in nature, or flat?
  • What are the key anticipated user paths throughout this content?

I sought to follow as closely as possible the paradigms I observed were familiar to people in CRE, knowing of course that none of my designs would fully stand the test of time and that future designers would curse my name.

Feedback from Design Partners

Prophia is an enterprise-level SaaS product; It isn’t cheap, it can’t be used instantly upon sign-up, and the decision to subscribe to it involves long sales cycles. For these reasons, the Prophia MVP could not have succeeded without our Design Partners—8 CRE firms of different sizes who provided monthly feedback. I ran (and continue to run) this program, geared toward:

  • Gathering feedback on features in design and on our roadmap
  • Shadowing team members to better understand workflows/processes
  • Access to sample critical business reports and documents

The Prophia MVP was released on September 18th, 2018. My ongoing research and validation work with Design Partners continues to inform our product prioritization, and we’ve seen steady quarterly growth in new customers and especially in expansions ever since.
